Cloud Storage Comparison: Google Drive vs. OneDrive vs. iCloud

Storing your files in the cloud is no longer optional; it's a necessity for backup and collaboration. But which service should you trust with your data? We compare the three biggest players.

Google Drive: The Versatile King

Google Drive remains the most popular choice due to its integration with Gmail and Google Docs. It offers 15GB of free storage, which is the most generous among the big three. It works perfectly on both Android and iOS.

Microsoft OneDrive: The Office Essential

If you use Word, Excel, or PowerPoint for work, OneDrive is the best choice. Its deep integration with Windows 11 and Microsoft 365 makes it the go-to for professionals. While its free tier is small (5GB), the paid plans offer incredible value when bundled with Office apps.

Apple iCloud: The Seamless Choice

For iPhone and Mac users, iCloud is the most convenient option. It works silently in the background, syncing your photos and device backups perfectly. However, it is difficult to use on Android or Windows compared to the other two.
