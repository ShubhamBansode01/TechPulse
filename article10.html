How to Build Your First Tech Blog and Earn with AdSense

February 28, 2026 By Tech Team 6 Min Read
[AdSense Top Banner Area]

If you love technology, sharing your knowledge through a blog is one of the most rewarding things you can do. Not only does it help others, but it can also become a steady source of passive income through Google AdSense.

Many beginners think you need to be a master coder to start a website. That is completely false! Today, setting up a professional, money-making tech blog is as easy as setting up a social media profile. Here is your step-by-step roadmap to getting started.

1. The Foundation: Domain and Hosting

To have a website, you need two things: an address and a piece of land.

  • Domain Name (The Address): This is what people type into their browser (like techpulse.com). Try to pick a name that is short, memorable, and ends in ".com" or ".in".
  • Web Hosting (The Land): This is a powerful computer where your website files are stored so people can access them 24/7. Companies like Hostinger or Bluehost offer great, affordable plans for beginners in India.

2. Choose Your Platform (Hint: Use WordPress)

While coding a website from scratch using HTML and CSS (like we did for this exact site!) is a great learning experience, the easiest way to launch a blog quickly is by using WordPress.

WordPress powers over 40% of the entire internet. It is free, requires zero coding knowledge, and has thousands of free themes. Most hosting providers have a "1-Click WordPress Install" button. Just click it, and your site is live!

[AdSense In-Article Banner Area]

3. Create the "Must-Have" Legal Pages

If you want Google AdSense to approve your blog for showing ads, your website must look like a legitimate business. Before you even apply, you absolutely must create these 4 pages:

  1. About Us: Tell your readers who you are and why you started the blog.
  2. Contact Us: Provide a form or an email address so people can reach you.
  3. Privacy Policy: Explains how your site uses cookies (AdSense requires this).
  4. Terms & Conditions: The legal rules of using your website.

Make sure links to these pages are clearly visible in your website's footer or top menu.

4. Write Original, High-Quality Content

This is where most beginners fail. You cannot simply copy and paste articles from other websites, and you shouldn't just let AI write everything without editing it. Google's systems are very smart and will reject your AdSense application for "Low-Value Content."

Aim to write at least 10 to 15 original articles (around 800 to 1500 words each) before applying for AdSense. Add your own opinions, break things down simply for your readers, and use high-quality images.

5. Apply for AdSense and Be Patient

Once your site has a good structure, legal pages, and 15 solid articles, go to the Google AdSense website and submit your URL. The review process usually takes anywhere from a few days to two weeks.

If you get rejected the first time, don't panic! Google will usually tell you why. Fix the issue, write a few more articles, and apply again. Consistency is the real secret to blogging success.
