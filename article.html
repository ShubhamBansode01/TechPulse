OnePlus Nord CE 4 vs Nothing Phone (2a): Which is Better?

April 9, 2026 By Tech Team 5 Min Read
[AdSense Top Banner Area]

If you are looking to buy a new smartphone this year, the ₹20,000 to ₹25,000 price range is the sweet spot. You get premium features without emptying your wallet. But with so many options, choosing the right one can be incredibly confusing.

Today, we are looking at the two most popular phones in this budget: the OnePlus Nord CE 4 and the Nothing Phone (2a). Both of these smartphones cost around ₹20,000 to ₹25,000 in India, but they are built for very different types of people.

1. Design & Build Quality

The OnePlus Nord CE 4 takes a safe and classic approach. It has a smooth, shiny plastic back that looks premium, especially in the "Celadon Marble" green color. It looks like a normal, beautiful smartphone that easily blends in.

The clean, traditional look of the OnePlus Nord CE 4.

The Nothing Phone (2a) is the complete opposite. It features a transparent back panel where you can see the "insides" of the phone's design. It also has Nothing’s famous "Glyph Interface"—which means there are actual LED lights on the back of the phone that light up when you get a call or a text message.

The unique, transparent Glyph design of the Nothing Phone (2a).

2. Performance (Gaming + Daily Use)

The OnePlus Nord CE 4 uses the Snapdragon 7 Gen 3. In beginner terms, this is a very powerful engine for this price. If you like playing heavy games like BGMI (PUBG) or Call of Duty, this phone will handle them beautifully without getting too hot.

The Nothing Phone (2a) uses the MediaTek Dimensity 7200 Pro. It is perfectly fast for opening apps, chatting on WhatsApp, and doing everyday tasks. It can play games too, but it isn't specifically designed for hardcore gaming like the OnePlus.

[AdSense In-Article Banner Area]

3. Final Verdict

The overall winner is the OnePlus Nord CE 4. When you are buying the best phone under ₹25,000, you want maximum value for your money. The OnePlus Nord CE 4 gives you a faster processor, a much larger battery, and importantly, it actually gives you a 100W super-fast charger in the box—all for a lower starting price than its competitor. It is a reliable, powerful, and complete package.

(That being said, if you truly care more about showing off a cool design and taking ultra-wide photos, and you don't mind spending extra on a charger, the Nothing Phone 2a is still a brilliant second choice).
